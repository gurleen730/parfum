Introduction

What is Perfume?

Perfume is a carefully crafted blend of essential oils, aroma compounds, fixatives, and alcohol — designed to give a person, object, or living space a pleasant scent.

But it is so much more than a product. It is a form of self-expression, a memory trigger, and a silent language. From ancient Egypt to modern luxury houses like Chanel and Dior, fragrance has always symbolized elegance, identity, and feeling.

"A woman who doesn't wear perfume has no future."

— Coco Chanel

Composition

Fragrance Notes

🍃 First impression

Top Notes

The opening burst — what you smell in the first 15–30 minutes. Light, fresh, and fleeting.

Lemon · Bergamot · Grapefruit
🌹 Heart of the scent

Middle Notes

The true character of the fragrance that emerges after the top notes fade, lasting 2–4 hours.

Rose · Lavender · Jasmine
🪵 Lasting depth

Base Notes

The deep, rich foundation that lingers for hours and gives the perfume its staying power.

Musk · Sandalwood · Vanilla

Concentration

Types of Perfume

Type Oil Concentration Lasts For
Parfum 20 – 40% 8 – 24 hours
Eau de Parfum 15 – 20% 5 – 8 hours
Eau de Toilette 5 – 15% 3 – 5 hours
Eau de Cologne 2 – 5% 1 – 3 hours

Scent Families

Fragrance Families

🌸

Floral

Soft, romantic, and timeless. Built around flower essences — rose, peony, lily. The most popular family in women's perfumery.

🌲

Woody

Warm, earthy, and grounding. Think cedarwood, oud, and vetiver — often the backbone of masculine fragrances.

🍋

Citrus

Bright, fresh, and uplifting. Perfect for everyday wear, combining lemon, orange, and neroli notes.

🔥

Oriental

Rich, exotic, and sensual. Blends of amber, spice, and resin that leave a deep, unforgettable impression.
